iterative work process

I focused on working iteratively when designing this logo. I first started off with approximately 10 ideas and chose two of the best ones based on an in-class group critique. Then, I made about 10 to 15 versions for each of the two ideas. After a 1:1 critique session with my professor, I chose one design out of 30+ versions and made additional versions of the design. Such repetitive process forced me to come up with as many ideas as I can and discover possibilities that I hadn’t thought about initially.

critique sessions

While working on this logo design, I had an opportunity to experience 3 types of critique session. First, the pinup. This critique session began by students pinning up their printed designs on the wall, allowing everyone to see their work all at once. Then students would go around the room and vote on the best one out of many designs that each student had come up with. Second, 1:1 critique. My second draft was review by my professor and it was productive to have someone professional pinpoint on what works, what doesn’t work, and what improvements can be made. Finally, design presentation. I would formally present my design to the class and my classmates would throw me advice and critiques.

brand identity

The first and the most important part of this project was coming up with a solid concept of the imaginary brand that was going to create. Having a good sense of brand identity allowed me to quickly come up with the brand’s style, image, and color palette. Since I created a clear and specific brand identity, I was also able to critique my own ideas and designs by questioning whether or not the design represents the brand’s goals.
